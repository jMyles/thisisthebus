With the 4th amendment as a vague memory

One of the worst downsides to living on a bus in the USA comes from the use of the home as a vehicle for traveling on the public roadways. You see, our government, through the intellectual pure and honest Supreme Court, has decided that vehicles enjoy almost none of the basic protections of the 4th amendment against unreasonable search and seizure.

Our experience yesterday was utterly unreasonable; no thoughtful adult can possibly conclude otherwise.

The trick works like this:

1) Find a pretext to conclude a vehicle traffic stop. In our case it was that the wheel of Chelsea's bicycle partly obstructed our license plate. 2) Bring a dog along to assert that there is an odor of some procribed object, such as plant matter from a criminal gardening ring.

Then, because (many years ago in x v x) the Supreme Court has ruled that probable cause alone is sufficient to conduct a complete search of the vehicle, the state can now trapse through our entire home, literally turning over every possession.

2017-03-11

High Falls State Park
Jackson, Georgia

We are, again, pleased with the Georgia State Park system. High Falls State Park is a beautiful & well maintained campground. We initially pulled into a site after the sun had set without knowing the layout of the land. The next morning, after Justin & Fibonacci took a bike ride to the park office and back, Fibonacci took a solo ride to find a playground that they had passed on their earlier journey. So, we moved the bus to a loop with the playground as well as other families with children. Nacci has enjoyed the slide now that he's figured out how to descend himself. Justin has worked a short gig, we've continued schooling ourselves on the JFK assassination and eaten yummy home-cooked meals. And continued watching the Star Trek Voyager series.
